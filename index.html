    Why be a cybersecuirty engineer?

    Cybersecurity engineering caught my eye due to the fact that it has multiple areas, roles and responsibilities. The role of a cybersecurity engineer is to identify threats and vulnerabilities in systems and software, then apply their knowledge and skills to develop and implement solutions to defend against hacking, malware and ransomware or to any inside threats and all types of cybercrime happening in a specific company. The responsibilities that fall under this job are Examining the organization's current security issues, Set up firewalls and intrusion detection systems, customizing them to meet the organization's demands, Analyzing threats and performing regular security audits, Putting in place an automated system within the company to guarantee that security policies are effective and efficient, investigating security breaches and put in place sound incident response strategies, learning from prior mistakes to develop even more robust security processes, Keeping up with industry standards and make sure you're in full compliance, Conducting tests and provide informative insights based on the results. Analyzing evolved security needs throughout a product's lifespan and ensuring that they are addressed at each step, Observing any changes inside the organization to ensure that security is not jeopardized, Outline the company's internal security rules, Recommend additional development to maintain the company in compliance with security best practices.
